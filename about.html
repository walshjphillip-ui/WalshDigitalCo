Most local businesses don't have a marketing problem. They have a coordination problem.

The website was built by one person, the logo by another, the photos came off a phone, and nobody has touched the Google listing since it was created. Every piece is fine on its own. Together they read like four different companies — and the business ends up looking smaller than it actually is.

I do all five services myself, which means they're decided together rather than negotiated between vendors. The photography is shot knowing where it lands on the site. The brand is drawn knowing what the feed needs. Nothing gets lost in a handoff, because there isn't one.
